Research Experience

ARMIS: Autonomous Mobile Sensing System for Intelligent Indoor Environmental Monitoring

First / Co-first Author
Indoor Air 2026 · Oral
Jul 2025 – Feb 2026
  • Built the ARMIS autonomous mobile robot platform integrating high-precision IEQ sensors and multimodal vision, addressing the limited spatial resolution and lack of occupant-awareness in conventional fixed sensors.
  • Applied Gaussian Process Regression (GPR) to reconstruct high-resolution IEQ fields (2 m × 2 m) from sparse sampling trajectories, capturing a 4.1 °C spatial gradient in temperature and CO₂ concentration hotspots.
  • Integrated YOLOv5 occupant detection with GPT-4o / GLM-4v vision-language models (VLMs) for non-intrusive extraction of clothing insulation and metabolic rate, feeding into the PMV model for personalized thermal comfort assessment.
  • Conducted a 14-day field experiment across 7,700 m² of public space in Tsinghua's C Building, collecting ~1,300 IEQ field measurements and ~1,100 scene images.
Robotics GPR VLMs IEQ Thermal Comfort

ArchEGraph: Geometry–Topology–Physics Aligned Building Energy Graph Dataset

Co-first Author (Equal Contribution)
Submitted to NeurIPS 2026
May 2025 – Present
  • Collaboration across Tsinghua × UC Berkeley × MIT.
  • Constructed a large-scale benchmark dataset comprising 5,481 buildings and 49,326 simulation cases, with 133,000 spatial nodes and 1.44 million surface nodes.
  • Represented buildings as heterogeneous graphs that align geometric meshes, spatial topology, meteorological data, and hourly zonal thermal loads — filling a critical gap in building energy modeling.
  • Defined two benchmark tasks — M2G (Mesh-to-Graph reconstruction) and G2E (Graph-to-Energy prediction) — with cross-building and cross-climate generalization protocols.
  • Led data preprocessing, building complexity parameterization, and GNN model development.
